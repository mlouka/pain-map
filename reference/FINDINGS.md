## The Key Finding: C5-C6 Corridor

Everything points to right-sided C5-C6 nerve root compression. Four nerves share this corridor, and each one maps to a specific symptom:

| Nerve | Origin | What it explains |
|-|-|
| Dorsal scapular nerve | C5 | Shoulder blade pain along medial border |
| Suprascapular nerve | C5-C6 | Deep shoulder/upper scapula pain |
| Axillary nerve | C5-C6 | Deltoid radiation |
| Musculocutaneous nerve | C5-C7 | Biceps radiation when severe |

All four pass through or near the scalene muscles in the neck — a known compression point.

## Why Head Position Matters

- Neck extension (head up) narrows the neural foramina from the back
- Right rotation narrows the RIGHT foramina specifically
- Combined = maximum compression on right C5-C6 roots
- This is exactly what Spurling's test replicates

## The Dorsal Scapular Nerve + Artery

- The DSN and dorsal scapular artery run together along the medial scapular border
- Both can be compressed at the middle scalene muscle
- If both are compressed: nerve irritation = sharp pain, reduced arterial flow = constant ache
- Two structures, two pain qualities — matches my experience
- 52.7% of patients with interscapular pain had evidence of DSN neuropathy (often misdiagnosed)
- Research says "manipulation is often met with temporary relief only" — matches my pattern

## Questions to Ask My Chiropractor

1. "Could this be C5-C6 foraminal compression on the right? The dorsal scapular nerve, suprascapular nerve, and brachial plexus superior trunk all originate from C5-C6 and could explain both my scapular pain and arm radiation."

2. "Can you evaluate C5 and C6 directly and adjust them if they need it? I want to test whether targeting those levels specifically makes a difference versus finding them through compensation checking."

3. "When the activator reproduces the pain going down my spine, what structure is that hitting?"

4. "Could both the dorsal scapular nerve AND artery be compressed at the scalenes?"

5. "Is there a pattern to which compensations are holding vs not?"

## Tests That Match My Symptoms

- **Spurling's test** — head extension + right rotation + downward pressure. If it reproduces arm pain, confirms cervical root compression. (My pain already worsens with this exact movement without the pressure.)
- **Shoulder abduction test (Bakody's sign)** — raise arm, hand on head. If pain decreases, confirms nerve root compression.
- **Upper limb tension test** — stretches the brachial plexus. If it reproduces arm radiation, confirms brachial plexus involvement.
- **Scalene compression** — press on scalene muscles. If it reproduces symptoms, confirms scalenes as the compression point.
