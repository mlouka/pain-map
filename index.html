Your Pain Zone

Sharp pain along the medial border of your right scapula (the inner edge of the shoulder blade). This is the rhomboid muscle area — the muscles running between the spine and the scapula bone.

Levator Scapulae

The muscle running from the upper cervical spine (C1-C4) down to the superior angle of the scapula (upper inner corner). Secondary tension point.

Nerve Origin: C3-C5

The dorsal scapular nerve originates from C3-C5 nerve roots in the cervical spine. On the model, look for the yellow nerve branches exiting the spine at neck level.

The Nerve Path

The dorsal scapular nerve pierces through the middle scalene muscle, then descends under the levator scapulae, running along the medial scapular border. This is why C-spine misalignment causes shoulder blade pain.

Why your adjustment works ~70%: When C1/C2 alignment is corrected, it relieves compensatory strain at C3-C5, decompressing the dorsal scapular nerve root. The 30% miss rate likely means the subluxation vector doesn't always decompress that specific nerve root.
